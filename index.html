WHO WE ARE?

A seed of an idea that was ~~ dreamt in 2017, germinated in 2019, was to make the world a better place. With each small and consistent step we tried to touch every visualized milestone.
Annant Unnati Foundation, a non government organization, is a team of knack and thrilled volunteers, working together with an aim to eradicate illiteracy, poverty and starving. We also strive for providing moral values and making every child a responsible citizen for a better India. With a united front, we fight to bring about a change in every sphere of life, to touch souls and change destinies.

WHAT WE BELIEVE IN?

I alone cannot change the world, but I can cast a stone across the waters to create many ripples.
-Mother Teresa

Our mission is to thrive for a world where every child attains the right to survive, education, protection, development and participation. We aim to bring about holistic change and cognitive thinking in young minds.
With this mission Annant Unnati Foundation was established in 2019, good intentions and noble approach always gets awarded, and on the opening day 74 children handed over their destiny in our hands. Within 6 months, the number increased to 350+ along with 40+ volunteers joining hands for the noble cause. In less than six months, another center was inaugurated with 97 children.

WHAT WE DO?

  • Providing quality education to the underprivileged children .
  • Promoting awareness towards a sound health among the children.
  • Facilitating vocational training to the children to reduce their skill gap which will help them in either getting employed or starting their own enterprise.
  • Promoting moral education among children so as to make them responsible citizens.
  • Educating children about the importance of a clean environment and its benefits.
  • Support underprivileged economically in terms of providing admissions in schools.
  • Provide inclusive education to children with special needs
  • Increase the number of school going children.

BE A PART OF THE CHANGE

MISSION ACCOMPLISHED

Project Aahaar

The corona pandemic turned many lives upside down. Due to imposition of lock-down thousands of people lost their livelihoods. Therefore, we came forward to help those in need and distributed dry ration as well as cooked meals to 2000+ families.

Project Care

This program aims to overcome one of the major public health challenges of maintaining oral hygiene. Many children lack the awareness of oral hygiene leading to severe health damages. This initiative aims to promote oral hygiene among children. We organized two free dental health check-up camps in span of an year. A team of four dentists assessed the oral hygiene of 215 children and distributed dental hygiene kits.

Project Aashayein

The objective of this project is to provide students an opportunity to express themselves through the means of different activities and focus on their all round personality development. Our foundation organizes annual cultural and sports programs and various dance, yoga, singing, painting and drama classes on weekends. This program has helped promote holistic development and cognitive skills in our children.

Project Vikas

Through this project, we aim to spread basic literacy in the society. We have taken several initiatives to achieve this, such as providing free education to over 200 underprivileged students and enrolling them in schools and colleges for higher education. We also provide books and stationery kits to our students to ensure quality education for all.

Project ODH

We conducted over 10 blanket distribution drive in the winter months. Our team of volunteers went around the NCR and distributed more than 500 blankets to the poor and homeless. We also conducted 5 clothes distribution drives in the region of Indirapuram, Ghaziabad. This ensured a smile on the faces of people struggling to fight the chilly winter.

Project SheSafe

Through our SheSafe Project we took the initiative to distribute 100,000 sanitary pads to the needy and organized awareness meetings among the backward communities in different regions. We Provided access to sanitary pads to at least 10,000 women. One thousand (1000) school girls received support through proper knowledge, awareness and education on menstruation and related hygiene practices.
